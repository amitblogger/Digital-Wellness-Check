Understand Your Usage

Are you spending more time on your phone than you'd like? This tool helps you evaluate your daily screen time based on a research-informed approach to digital habits. Select apps/websites you use, enter the hours spent on them, and get a calculated **Dependence Score** from 0 to 100.

The scoring methodology goes beyond simple duration. It incorporates **app category weights** derived from behavioral insights into engagement patterns (like infinite scroll, autoplay, variable rewards) that can contribute differently to habit formation and potential dependence. Furthermore, the calculation considers the **total volume of time** spent, as even low-engagement activities can impact well-being if they consume excessive hours. By accounting for both the *nature* and the *volume* of your digital activities, the tool provides a more nuanced estimate of your dependence level.

Based on your score, you'll see which estimated risk band you fall into—from "Healthy Use" to "Addicted"—and receive simple, actionable tips to help you build healthier digital habits and reclaim your time. This tool is for self-assessment and awareness; it is not a substitute for professional medical or psychological advice.

Tips Based on Your Score

Your calculated score provides an estimated current dependence level, serving as a starting point for cultivating healthier digital habits. The tips below offer general suggestions for each estimated risk band. Keep in mind that building better digital habits is an ongoing process, and implementing even small, consistent changes can lead to significant improvements over time. Use your specific score result from the calculator for tailored recommendations.

Healthy Use (Score 0-25)

Excellent! Your screen time habits appear well-balanced and are likely not interfering negatively with other aspects of your life, such as work, relationships, or physical health. Maintaining this healthy balance requires ongoing awareness and conscious effort, especially as technology evolves and daily routines change. Celebrate your positive habits!

  • Continue practicing mindful technology use and regularly check in with your usage patterns to stay aware.
  • Implement short, regular screen breaks, particularly during extended periods of work or leisure spent online.
  • Prioritize and protect your time for offline activities, hobbies, exercise, and face-to-face social interactions.
  • Maintain control over your attention by regularly reviewing and disabling non-essential app notifications that can trigger impulsive phone checking.

Moderate Dependence (Score 26-50)

Your screen time is reaching a level where it could potentially reduce the time available for other valuable life activities like sleep, physical exercise, or in-person connections. This is a crucial stage to implement conscious strategies and set boundaries to prevent potentially problematic habits from developing further. Small adjustments now can make a big difference.

  • Designate specific "screen-free" times and physical zones in your home (e.g., no phones at the dinner table, devices off limits in the bedroom for an hour before sleep).
  • Aggressively manage notifications: turn off non-essential alerts for many apps to reduce constant distractions and lessen the urge to pick up your phone impulsively.
  • Actively schedule time to replace digital activities with offline pursuits you enjoy or have been meaning to start. Aim to swap at least 30 minutes to an hour daily.
  • Explore features like grayscale mode or Do Not Disturb settings on your phone; reducing visual stimuli can make apps less inherently attention-grabbing and appealing.
  • Utilize your phone's built-in screen time tracking features to get a more accurate picture of where your time is spent versus your estimates.

High Dependence (Score 51-75)

Screen time at this level is likely having a noticeable negative impact on various aspects of your daily life, such as academic/work productivity, sleep quality, personal relationships, or mental well-being. It indicates that technology use may be causing significant functional impairment. Taking serious, proactive steps to regain control is essential at this stage.

  • Implement and adhere to strict daily time limits using your phone's operating system features (Digital Wellbeing/Screen Time) or third-party app blocking tools for the specific applications where you tend to lose the most time unproductively.
  • Create clear boundaries between your digital presence and your personal, family, and work/study time. Avoid passive phone checking or scrolling during important activities or conversations.
  • Make your bedroom a completely phone-free sanctuary to protect your sleep hygiene. Invest in a traditional alarm clock.
  • Increase your self-awareness by identifying the specific emotional or situational triggers that lead you to excessive phone use (e.g., boredom, stress, anxiety, loneliness, social comparison). Journaling can be helpful for this.
  • Practice delay gratification: When you feel an impulsive urge to open a distracting app, intentionally wait for 5-10 minutes and try to engage in a different, non-digital activity first.

Addicted (Score 76-100)

A score in this range strongly suggests that your screen time habits are significantly impairing important areas of your life, displaying characteristics often associated with behavioral addiction. This level of dependence is serious and likely requires significant effort and potentially external support to address effectively. Recognizing the impact and seeking help are critical steps.

  • Acknowledge the severity of the issue and its impact on your life without judgment. Digital addiction is a complex challenge, and recognizing the need for help is a sign of strength and self-awareness.
  • It is highly recommended to seek professional support from a qualified therapist, counselor, or addiction specialist who has experience with behavioral addictions. They can provide tailored strategies, coping mechanisms, and support structures.
  • Consider implementing drastic measures if necessary: This might include temporarily or permanently deleting highly problematic apps, using aggressive blocking software (like Freedom or Cold Turkey - *external tools*) that make access difficult during set times, or even switching to a less-capable phone temporarily.
  • Communicate openly and honestly with trusted friends and family members about your struggles and your goals for reducing usage. Allowing them to provide support and accountability can be invaluable.
  • Actively schedule and commit to non-digital activities that bring you joy, fulfillment, or relaxation. Plan your day to include exercise, hobbies, social interaction, or time in nature to fill the void left by reduced screen time.
  • Explore the 'Resources' section in the footer for potential sources of support, information, and professional help.

About This Tool

This Digital Wellness Check tool was created to help individuals gain insightful awareness into their daily mobile screen time habits and understand the potential impact on their overall well-being. In our increasingly digital world, cultivating mindful and intentional technology use is paramount for health and balance. This tool is a starting point on that journey.

The calculator utilizes a **research-informed scoring system** designed to provide a more nuanced estimate than simply measuring total screen time. It incorporates **app category weights** based on behavioral science principles regarding digital engagement and habit formation. Different types of apps and online activities are assigned weights reflecting factors known to contribute to prolonged or automatic usage, such as the presence of endless scrolling feeds, autoplay video, variable reward systems common in social media and games, and the difference between passive consumption versus active creation or communication.

Crucially, the scoring also accounts for the **total volume of screen time**, recognizing that even activities with lower inherent addiction potential can become problematic if they consume an excessive number of hours daily. By blending the *type* of activity (weighted hours) with the *total duration* (raw hours), the tool aims to offer a more comprehensive, though still simplified, perspective on potential digital dependence.

**Important Disclaimer:** This tool provides an *estimated* dependence score solely for the purpose of self-assessment and raising awareness. It is not a clinical diagnostic instrument. Digital addiction is a complex behavioral health issue, and any concerns about your screen time usage, its impact on your life, or your mental health should be discussed with a qualified healthcare professional, therapist, or counselor. We encourage you to use the results as a prompt for reflection and positive change.

Privacy Policy

Your privacy is of the utmost importance to us. This Digital Wellness Check tool has been developed with a strong commitment to your data security and confidentiality. The tool operates entirely within your web browser using client-side JavaScript. This means that all calculations, the data you input (such as app selections and hours), and the resulting dependence score are processed and stored only on your local device (your computer or smartphone) temporarily while the page is open.

Crucially, **no data entered into the calculator is ever transmitted over the internet to our server, collected, stored in any database, or shared with any third parties.** There is no backend system collecting your personal usage information. Once you close or refresh the web page, all the input data you provided is automatically cleared from your device's memory. You can therefore use this tool with complete confidence and peace of mind, knowing that your personal screen time usage patterns and results remain entirely private and anonymous.

Further Resources & Help

If your score from the Digital Wellness Check indicates a potential for high dependence or addiction, or if you are finding it challenging to manage your screen time habits effectively, please know that you are not alone and there are many resources available to provide support and guidance. Taking the step to seek help is a sign of strength and a crucial part of moving towards healthier digital well-being.

Consider exploring the following types of resources:

  • Professional Help: Licensed therapists, counselors, psychologists, or clinical social workers specializing in behavioral addictions or technology overuse can provide personalized assessment, therapy (like CBT), and coping strategies tailored to your specific situation.
  • Digital Wellbeing Tools & Apps: Explore the features built into your smartphone's operating system (e.g., Digital Wellbeing on Android, Screen Time on iOS) for tracking, limiting, and setting downtime. Consider third-party apps designed for focus, blocking distracting websites/apps, or scheduling digital detox periods (examples include Freedom, Forest, Cold Turkey - *mentioning these are examples of external apps, not specific endorsements*).
  • Educational Materials: Increase your understanding of digital habits and their impact by reading books, articles, and research from reputable sources on screen time, dopamine, persuasive design, and the psychology of technology use.
  • Support Communities: Connecting with others who are working towards similar goals can be incredibly supportive. Look for online forums, social media groups, or local community groups focused on digital minimalism, digital detox, or managing technology use.

Taking proactive steps to address problematic screen time is an important investment in your mental, physical, and social well-being. You have the power to change your relationship with technology.
